# Google Summer of Code - Contributor Guide

If you are a contributor interested in participating in the Google Summer of Code with the XeroCodee community, please visit the GSOC idea list. - [Google Summer Of Code](./gsoc2023-idea.md)

---

**What should you do before to get accepted:**

- Check out the program [website](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/).
- Understand how the program [works](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/how-it-works).
- Read the [contributor guide](https://google.github.io/gsocguides/student/) of the program.
- Make sure you are [eligible](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/terms/contributor) to participate in the program.
- Interact with the community using the communication channels.
- Browse the [idea list](./gsoc2023-idea.md). The earlier, the better.

---

**Consider the following factors while selecting a project:**

1. You have met at least half of the prerequisites. The remaining abilities may be polished on the go, so don't be concerned if you lack any.

2. You can relate to the project concept and have utilised something relevant to it as a user.

3. You are more interested in gaining new abilities than in bragging about the ones you currently have.

---

**Technical skills**

Experience building and implementing web applications, as well as managing end-to-end client and server-side development, is required. It is necessary to have knowledge of a prominent Javascript web framework (React.js, Next.js, Vue.js, Typescript, and so on). The selection of the UI library/tools is flexible and will be dependent on the mentor and mentee's agreement.

---

**Contribution Process**

__Step 1:__ Learn about the goal, sub-goals, and the required skills of the idea you are interested in from the [idea list](./gsoc2023-idea.md).

__Step 2:__ Get in touch with the mentors of the project to ask them questions, show them your interest, and find out whether they're accepting donations.

__Step 3:__ Write a proposal detailing your ideas, approach, and the results you expect to achieve with your contribution. The GSOC website should be used to submit the proposal.

__Step 4:__ If your proposal is selected, a mentor will be assigned to you to help you out with the contribution procedure.

__Step 5:__ Get on with the development in accordance with your idea, using the established coding standards and development procedures.

__Step 6:__ Ask your mentor for feedback on a regular basis, and be open to constructive criticism and feedback.

__Step 7:__ Whenever you're ready , submit your updated code for review. Your mentor will review the modifications and provide feedback.

__Step 8:__ Once the modifications are approved, it'll be integrated into the primary codebase.

__Step 9:__ Your work will be assessed by your mentor and other project contributors at the end of the GSOC period.

**Code of Conduct**

Xerocodee is committed to fostering an environment that is inviting and inclusive for all participants, including volunteers, contributors, and employees. We anticipate that all participants will abide by the following code of conduct in order to maintain a positive and productive environment:

- We expect all participants to treat one another with respect and to abstain from any kind of abuse, bias, or harassment. For instance, personal attacks, bullying, and hate speech are not tolerated in our organization.
- All participants are encouraged to communicate in a respectful and clear manner. This involves refraining from using words or actions that are demeaning, disrespectful, or otherwise inappropriate.
- All contributions are valued and respected in our collaborative and inclusive workplace. We appreciate constructive criticism and feedback as well as varied viewpoints and experiences.
- We respect the confidentiality of sensitive or confidential information, and we expect that all participants will maintain the necessary level of informational confidentiality.
- In the event of a dispute, we urge the contributors to address the matter in a fair and helpful way. We have a procedure in place to settle disputes if a resolution can't be reached.
- We provide a clear process for reporting the issue if a participant sees or experiences inappropriate behaviour.
