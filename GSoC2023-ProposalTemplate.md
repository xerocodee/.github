### **Proposal**
You will be evaluated based on your project proposal. Write a detailed proposal outlining what you want to achieve, the scope of your project, and why we should hire you. Proposals are the foundation of GSoC projects and hence one of the most critical things to get right. The proposal not only influences our selection on which contributor to hire, but it also influences Google's decision on how many contributor slots to give to XeroCodee.


**Below is the application template:**

**Introduction**

Every software project should aim to tackle a specific problem. You should define the problem before giving a solution (your Google Summer of Code project). What is the current situation of affairs? What is the problem you want to tackle, and why? Then finish with a phrase or two explaining your answer. If required, include links to discussions, features, or bugs that further characterise the problem.

**Project goals**

Be brief and to the point, and consider formatting it as a list. Make a detailed list of deliverables, outlining exactly what you want to achieve and what you do not intend to do. "Future developments" can be discussed, but what matters is your commitment to the Google Summer of Code period.

**Implementation**

Be specific. Describe your strategy for resolving the problem you identified above. Include technical facts to demonstrate your understanding of the technology. Draw in reasonable depth the essential technical features of your proposed solution. Include unit testing and code documentation throughout the coding process. These important parts cannot be left until the program's final weeks. If user documentation, apidox, or similar is necessary, it should be written throughout each week rather than at the end.

**Timeline**

Demonstrate that you understand the problem, have a solution, have broken it down into manageable portions, and have a practical strategy for achieving your objective. Expectations are set here, so don't make promises you can't maintain. A reasonable, realistic, and thorough timeframe is preferable than promising the unattainable.

If you have any other obligations during GSoC, such as a job, vacation, examinations, an internship, seminars, or papers to write, please list them here. GSoC should be viewed as a full-time job, with a weekly workload of roughly 40 hours. If you have disagreements, describe how you plan to resolve them. If it is discovered that you have conflicts that you did not declare, you may be failed.

It is critical to communicate openly and clearly. Include your communication strategies in your proposal, preferably on a regular basis. You must commence weekly formal communication, such as a blog post on the agreed-upon location. Failure will occur from a lack of communication.

**About me**

Provide your contact details (Phone, Discord ID etc) and a few phrases about yourself and why you believe you are the ideal candidate for this position. Prior contributions to XeroCodee are necessary; please list them. Name individuals (other developers, students, or instructors) who can serve as references for you. If required, mention your topic of study. Join the relevant discord/telegram/slack channels, mail lists, and blog feeds now. Not only do we want you to contribute code, but we also want you to be a part of our community.

*Tell us whether you're submitting ideas to other organisations and if you'd select XeroCodee if you had the option.*

Consider the following:

- Are you comfortable working independently for a supervisor or mentor who is thousands of kilometres and maybe 12 time zones away? How will you collaborate with your mentor to track your progress? Have you already worked in this style?

- Are you comfortable working closely with a supervisor whose native language is English if English is not your first language? What is your native language, since it may assist us discover a mentor who speaks the same language as you?

- You should get your proposal assessed when you have finished writing it. Do not rely on the XeroCodee mentors to do it for you via the online interface, but we will make every effort to comment on each idea. It is a good idea to have a coworker or a developer review your plan. Clarity and thoroughness are essential.

## **Hints**

**Submit your idea early:** Developers pay greater attention to proposals that are submitted early since they have more time to read them. The more people who view your proposal, the more likely it is to be debated.

**Do not leave everything to the last minute:** since Google is running the website, it is prudent to anticipate a last-minute overload on the server. So, make sure you send in your application and evidence of enrollment before the deadline. Also, applications filed late will receive the least attention from mentors, therefore you may receive a lower vote as a result. Submitting a draught early allows for comments from potential mentors.

**Maintain simplicity:** Be succinct and precise. Give your article a descriptive title. **"My Project"** is the worst title imaginable!

**Understand what you're talking about:** Do not submit suggestions that cannot be completed in a summer or are unrelated to XeroCodee. If your proposal is uncommon, explain why you picked XeroCodee as your mentoring organisation.
There may be special reasons to accept a proposal that cannot be completed over the summer if it is evident that there will be commitment beyond the summer time or the project may be well split into sub-projects. If you choose to take that route, your proposal must be extremely legible in order for us to analyse the modifications of a project as it goes through various coding programmes.

**Aim high:** submit many proposals. You may also submit your application to another organisation. If you submit more than one idea, please tell us which one you would pick if both were chosen. Former students would urge you to focus on one or two killer ideas rather than three.
